Take a few minutes to exercise your data access rights. Under GDPR (Article 15) and CCPA (Section 1798.100), you have a legal right to access the personal data companies hold about you. Seeing your own data is worth a thousand abstractions.

**YouTube / Google:**
1. Go to [myactivity.google.com](https://myactivity.google.com)
2. Sign in with your Google account
3. Click "YouTube History"
4. Scroll through the timeline

This is every single video you have watched on that account. Every search query. With dates and timestamps. Many users find years of history here. For a comprehensive export, use [takeout.google.com](https://takeout.google.com) -- Google Takeout lets you download your complete data across all Google services.

**TikTok:**
1. Open TikTok > tap Profile
2. Tap the three lines (menu) > Settings and Privacy
3. Tap Account > Download Your Data
4. Request your data and wait for the file

You will receive a structured file containing your complete TikTok history -- every video watched, every like, every comment, every search, plus device information and inferred interests. Processing can take several days.

**Instagram / Meta:**
1. Go to Settings > Accounts Center > Your Information and Permissions
2. Tap "Download Your Information"
3. Select your account and request a download

The export includes your messages, searches, ad interactions, and -- critically -- a list of interest categories Meta has assigned to you. That interest taxonomy is particularly revealing and often contains inferences you would not have expected.

**Spotify:**
1. Go to [spotify.com/account](https://spotify.com/account)
2. Click Privacy Settings
3. Click "Download Your Data"

You will receive your streaming history, search queries, and the inferences Spotify has drawn about your listening behavior.

**Data hygiene: practical steps.**

Google allows you to configure auto-deletion policies. Go to myactivity.google.com > Activity controls > choose to auto-delete data older than 3 months, 18 months, or 36 months. This limits the historical depth of your behavioral profile.

On TikTok, you can clear your watch history: Profile > Menu > Settings and Privacy > Content Preferences > Clear watch history. This effectively resets your recommendation model.

For broader data broker exposure, check services like [haveibeenpwned.com](https://haveibeenpwned.com) for breach exposure, and consider opting out of major data brokers (Acxiom, Spokeo, WhitePages) -- though this is an ongoing maintenance task, not a one-time fix.

**Consider this:** Did the volume and granularity of collected data surprise you? Most people -- including technical professionals -- underestimate the scope of what is stored. Exercising your data access rights is not just an educational exercise; it is the foundation for making informed decisions about which services deserve your data and under what terms.
