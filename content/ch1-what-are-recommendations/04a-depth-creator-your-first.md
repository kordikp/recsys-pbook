You don't need a computer to build a recommendation system. You just need some friends, a pencil, and a piece of paper. Let's do this!

## What You Need
- 5 friends (or family members)
- Paper and a pencil
- 5 minutes

## Step 1: The Survey

Ask each friend: **"What are your top 3 movies (or shows)?"**

Write down their answers. It might look like this:

| Person | Movie 1 | Movie 2 | Movie 3 |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| Emma | Encanto | Spider-Verse | Moana |
| Liam | Spider-Verse | Avengers | Star Wars |
| Mia | Encanto | Moana | Frozen |
| Noah | Avengers | Star Wars | Spider-Verse |
| Ava | Moana | Encanto | Luca |

## Step 2: Find the Connections

Now look for patterns. Draw lines between people who share a movie:
- Emma and Liam both like Spider-Verse
- Emma and Mia both like Encanto AND Moana
- Liam and Noah both like Spider-Verse, Avengers, AND Star Wars

Emma and Mia share TWO movies. They have very similar taste!

## Step 3: Make Recommendations

Here's the magic moment. Emma hasn't seen Frozen, but Mia loves it. And Emma and Mia have super similar taste. So you recommend **Frozen to Emma**.

Liam hasn't seen Star Wars... wait, actually he has. But Noah hasn't seen Spider-Verse... he has too. Hmm. Look for movies that one person loves but a similar person HASN'T seen.

Noah likes Avengers and Star Wars (action/adventure). Liam likes those too, plus Spider-Verse. Noah already has Spider-Verse. But what about Liam? He doesn't have Encanto or Moana -- but those don't match his style. See? You're already thinking like a recommender!

## Step 4: Test It

Tell your friends your recommendations. Ask them: "Would you actually watch this?" Keep score. How many did you get right?

**Try this!** Do the same experiment but with songs, games, or even pizza toppings. Does the method work better for some things than others?

Congratulations -- you just built a recommendation system using the exact same idea that powers Netflix. Theirs just does it with 200 million people instead of 5.
