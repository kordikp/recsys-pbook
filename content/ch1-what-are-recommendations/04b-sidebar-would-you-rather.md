Time for a game. For each question, pick one side. Be honest -- there are **no right answers**. (Seriously. Engineers at big tech companies argue about these exact questions all the time.)

## Round 1: Surprise vs. Accuracy

**Would you rather** get recommendations that are PERFECT every time (but you're never surprised by anything new) **OR** sometimes get weird recommendations (but occasionally discover something absolutely amazing you never would have found)?

Think about it. If the system only gives you what it KNOWS you like, you'll never discover new interests. But if it guesses too wildly, you'll waste a lot of time on stuff you hate.

## Round 2: Privacy vs. Personalization

**Would you rather** have a recommendation system that knows EVERYTHING about you (super accurate, almost reads your mind) **OR** one that knows almost nothing about you (way more private, but the suggestions are pretty generic)?

This one is tough. More data means better recommendations. But more data also means some company knows a LOT about you.

## Round 3: Design Challenge

**Would you rather** design recommendations for a **music app** OR a **restaurant app**? Why?

Hint: think about how often you listen to the same song vs. how often you eat at the same restaurant. That difference changes everything about how you'd build the system.

## The Big Secret

These aren't just fun questions -- they're real **trade-offs** that engineers wrestle with every single day. There's no perfect answer. Every choice has a cost. And that's what makes building recommendation systems so interesting.

Discuss your answers with a friend. Did you pick the same sides?
