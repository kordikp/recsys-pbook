# What Sounds Most Fun to You?

You've learned the basics of what recommender systems are. Now it's time to figure out what YOU want to explore next. There's no wrong answer here -- this just helps you pick your adventure.

**Read each option and pick the one that excites you the most:**

---

**A) "Show me how YouTube works inside!"**
You want to peek behind the curtain. How does TikTok's algorithm actually decide what to show you? What does Spotify measure when you skip a song? You want diagrams, examples, and step-by-step breakdowns of real systems.

*You're an **Explorer**. You'll love the Explorer depth cards throughout this book.*

---

**B) "I want to build my own recommendation system!"**
Forget reading about it -- you want to MAKE one. Surveys, spreadsheets, maybe even some code. You want projects you can actually do, things you can show your friends.

*You're a **Creator**. Look for the Creator depth cards -- they're full of hands-on activities and projects.*

---

**C) "I want to understand WHY recommendations sometimes go wrong."**
You're curious about the big questions. Why do some recommendations feel creepy? What happens when the system gets it wrong? Is it fair? You like thinking deeply about how things work and what they mean.

*You're a **Thinker**. The Thinker depth cards will take you into the fascinating "why" behind everything.*

---

**D) "All of it! I want everything!"**
You can't pick just one? Perfect. Read the main spine of the book plus ALL the depth cards. You'll get the full picture.

*You're a **Universal** reader. Enjoy the whole book!*

---

**Remember:** you can always change your mind. Start as an Explorer and switch to Creator whenever you want. The book works no matter which path you take.
