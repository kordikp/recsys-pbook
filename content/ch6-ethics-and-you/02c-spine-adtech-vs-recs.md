You looked at sneakers on one website. Now sneaker ads follow you everywhere — Instagram, YouTube, news sites. That's creepy. But is it a recommendation system?

**No.** It's **adtech** — advertising technology. And the difference matters.

## Recommender Systems: Helping You Within One Service

A **recommender system** works within a single service to improve YOUR experience:
- Spotify suggests songs based on what you've listened to **on Spotify**
- Netflix suggests shows based on what you've watched **on Netflix**
- A bookstore recommends books based on what you've browsed **in that store**

The data stays within one platform. The goal is to help you find things you'll enjoy. When it works well, you think "This app really gets me!"

## AdTech: Tracking You Across the Internet

**Ad technology** works completely differently:
- It tracks you across MANY websites using cookies, pixels, and fingerprinting
- It builds a profile of your behavior across the entire internet
- It sells access to your attention to the highest bidder

That "abandoned cart" email you get after leaving shoes in an online store? That's a retargeting system — it followed you from the store to your inbox, sometimes through third-party data brokers.

## The Cross-Site Tracking Problem

Here's what happens behind the scenes:

1. You visit a shoe website
2. A tracking pixel from an ad network records: "User #4829 looked at Nike Air Max"
3. You go to a news website — the SAME ad network has a pixel there
4. It recognizes you: "This is User #4829 who wanted sneakers"
5. You see a sneaker ad on a completely unrelated news article

The shoe website, the news site, and the ad network are three different companies. Your data traveled between all of them — often without you realizing.

## Why This Matters

According to [Recombee's research](https://www.recombee.com/blog/modern-recommender-systems-part-1-introduction), recommender systems typically only use your anonymous interactions within one website. AdTech platforms, on the other hand, conduct large-scale profiling across multiple sites.

**The test**: Is this helping me find something I want on THIS website? That's recommendation. Is this following me to sell me something I already decided not to buy? That's adtech.

Both use algorithms. Both analyze your behavior. But their goals, their data practices, and their impact on your privacy are fundamentally different.
