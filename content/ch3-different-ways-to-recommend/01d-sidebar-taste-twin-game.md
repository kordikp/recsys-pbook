You're about to do what a collaborative filtering algorithm does -- but with your actual brain, on your actual friends.

## What You Need

- At least 3 friends (more is better!)
- Paper and something to write with
- 5 minutes

## How To Play

**Step 1:** Everyone secretly writes down their **top 3 favorite movies** (or shows, or games -- pick one category). Don't peek at each other's answers!

**Step 2:** Everyone reveals their lists at the same time.

**Step 3:** Compare. Count how many titles overlap between YOUR list and each friend's list.

- **3 matches** = Unbelievable. You share a brain.
- **2 matches** = Very strong taste twin!
- **1 match** = Some overlap. Promising.
- **0 matches** = Opposite twins. Still useful! (See below.)

**Step 4:** Your friend with the most overlap is your **taste twin**. Now ask them: what's a movie you love that WASN'T on my list?

That movie? It's your recommendation. A real, human-powered recommendation.

## Why This Works

You just ran collaborative filtering with your brain. The logic is identical to what Netflix and Spotify do:

1. Find people with similar taste (your taste twin)
2. See what THEY liked that YOU haven't tried yet
3. Recommend it

The only difference? Netflix does this with 230 million users instead of 3 friends. Same idea, impossibly bigger scale.

## Bonus Round

What about the friend with ZERO overlap? They might still be useful. If they hated a movie you hated, you agree on what's BAD -- which is also a pattern. Some recommendation systems use negative matches too!
